About Me

Welcome to my homepage. I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Human Development and Family Studies at Michigan State University. My research examines how teacher-child interactions both shape and are shaped by teachers' characteristics within early childhood education (ECE), and how these interactions influence children's development. I work closely with Dr. Claire D. Vallotton and Dr. Holly Brophy-Herb.

This program of research is organized around three interrelated themes: (1) evaluating the quality of teacher-child interactions and their implications for children's socioemotional development; (2) investigating how teachers' own characteristics (including their family and cultural backgrounds, as well as school experiences) inform teachers' practices; and (3) exploring how teacher-child interactions can be catalyzed to shape teachers' own development as professionals.

Ultimately, my work is intended to inform teacher preparation and ongoing professional support by providing an evidence base for targeted training that strengthens reflective capacity in order to help educators recognize how their own backgrounds and dispositions enter the classroom, and equips them with tools to intentionally refine practice and improve interaction quality.
